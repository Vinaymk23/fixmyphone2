10 Tips to Extend Your Phone's Battery Life

Is your phone constantly running out of juice? Follow these simple steps to get the most out of every charge.

Share this article:

We've all been there: you're out and about, and you see that dreaded red battery icon. A dying phone can be more than an inconvenience; it can be a real problem. While batteries naturally degrade over time, your habits can significantly impact their daily performance and long-term health. Here are 10 expert tips to help you extend your phone's battery life.

1. Lower Your Screen Brightness

The display is one of the biggest power drains on any smartphone. Lowering your screen brightness is the single most effective way to conserve battery. Better yet, enable "Auto-Brightness," which allows your phone to adjust the screen based on ambient light, saving power automatically.

2. Use Dark Mode

If your phone has an OLED or AMOLED screen (common in most modern iPhones and high-end Androids), using Dark Mode can save a surprising amount of battery. Unlike LCD screens, OLED displays don't light up black pixels, so a darker interface means less power consumption.

3. Reduce Screen Timeout

Set your screen to turn off after a short period of inactivity, like 30 seconds or one minute. Every moment your screen is on unnecessarily is a moment it's draining your battery.

4. Turn Off Unnecessary Notifications

Every time your phone buzzes and lights up with a notification, it uses power. Go into your settings and disable push notifications for non-essential apps. This will not only save battery but also help you focus.

5. Manage Location Services

Many apps track your location in the background, which is a major battery hog. In your privacy settings, review which apps have access to your location and change their permission to "While Using the App" or "Never."

6. Disable Background App Refresh

This feature allows apps to update their content in the background so they're up-to-date when you open them. While convenient, it constantly consumes power. Turn it off for apps that don't need to be constantly refreshed, like social media or games.

7. Use Wi-Fi Instead of Mobile Data

A cellular connection uses significantly more power than a Wi-Fi connection. Whenever possible, connect to a trusted Wi-Fi network to browse the internet, stream content, or download files.

8. Enable Low Power Mode

Both iOS and Android have a "Low Power" or "Battery Saver" mode. This feature automatically reduces background activity, lowers brightness, and makes other tweaks to maximize your remaining charge. It's a lifesaver when you're running low.

9. Avoid Extreme Temperatures

Exposing your phone to extreme heat or cold can permanently damage the battery's capacity. Avoid leaving your phone in a hot car or in direct sunlight for extended periods.

10. Consider a Battery Replacement

If you've tried everything and your battery life is still poor, the battery itself may be old and degraded. This is normal after a few years of use.
